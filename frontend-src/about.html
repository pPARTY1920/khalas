How it started
Born in Kampala,
built for Africa

In 2021, our founders — three university friends sitting in a Kampala apartment — got tired of waiting over an hour for food that sometimes never arrived. They saw a massive gap: Africa's food scene was vibrant and world-class, but the delivery infrastructure was broken.

So they built Zesto. Starting with just four restaurant partners and a fleet of two motorbike riders, the first week brought in 38 orders. By the end of month three, they had crossed 5,000.

"We didn't set out to build a tech company. We set out to make sure no one in Africa ever had to eat bad food, slowly." — Amara Diallo, Co-founder & CEO

Today, Zesto operates in 12 cities across East and West Africa, partnering with everything from street food legends to fine-dining restaurants — and we're just getting started.

What drives us
Our mission & values
Three principles that guide every decision we make, every rider we hire, and every partner we welcome.
Speed without sacrifice
We obsess over delivery time, but never at the cost of food quality or rider safety. Our routing technology ensures the fastest path while keeping everyone safe on the road.
Local first, always
We actively champion local restaurants, helping them reach new customers and compete in a digital-first world. 80% of our partner restaurants are independently owned businesses.
Riders are partners
Our riders are the backbone of Zesto. We offer competitive pay, health coverage, flexible hours, and a pathway to business ownership through our rider accelerator program.

Our principles
What we stand for
These aren't just words on a wall. They're the lens through which we make every decision.
01
Obsess over the customer
Every feature, every policy, every rider hire starts with one question: does this make the customer experience better? We read every review, respond to every complaint, and celebrate every 5-star moment.
02
Move fast, stay careful
Speed is in our name and our DNA. But we never rush decisions that affect safety, fairness, or trust. We ship quickly and iterate constantly — but we think deeply before we act.
03
Lift the community
Zesto exists because Africa's communities are extraordinary. We invest 5% of every delivery fee into local food entrepreneurship programs, school meal initiatives, and rider welfare funds.
04
Radical transparency
We publish our delivery data, our rider pay structure, and our environmental impact reports quarterly. If something goes wrong, we say so publicly and explain how we're fixing it.
Our journey
From apartment to Africa
Three years of growth, challenges, and a lot of delivered meals.
2021
Zesto is born 🚀
Three friends launch Zesto in Kampala with 4 restaurant partners and 2 riders. First 38 orders in week one. They sleep on the office floor to handle the night shift.
2022
First million orders 🎉
Expanded to Nairobi and Accra. Crossed 1 million orders delivered. Launched the Zesto rider app with live GPS tracking. Raised Series A funding of $4.2M.
2023
West Africa expansion 🌍
Launched in Lagos, Abidjan, and Dakar. Introduced mobile money payments, grocery delivery, and the Zesto Pro loyalty program. Hit 500,000 registered users.
2024
12 cities, 1,200+ partners 🤝
Operating in 12 cities with over 1,200 restaurant partners. Launched the Rider Accelerator Program, helping 200 riders start their own small businesses. Rated #1 food delivery app in East Africa.
2025
The journey continues ⚡
We're building toward 25 cities by end of year, launching Zesto for Business, and investing in electric rider vehicles to reduce our carbon footprint across every city we serve.
